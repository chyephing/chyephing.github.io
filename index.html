About

I am a PhD candidate at Sunway University working at the intersection of artificial intelligence and virology. My research focuses on predicting disease outcomes in chronic hepatitis B virus (HBV) infection using deep learning applied to viral genomes. With a background spanning computer science, life sciences, and medical practice, I aspire to bridge computational innovation and biomedical science to tackle pressing global health challenges.

Research

My doctoral project develops semi-supervised deep learning pipelines to predict HBV clinical outcomes, leveraging >11,000 genome sequences. Key components include:

  • GAN-based data augmentation to adress limited data size and class imbalance.
  • Semi-supervised DNN for genomic prediction.
  • Benchmarking against traditional machine learning models.
  • Preliminary results: ~85% accuracy in predicting disease outcomes.

This work relies on GPU acceleration for scalable genomic analysis. Future extensions of this research could be supported by healthcare AI platforms such as NVIDIA's Clara Parabricks and BioNeMo, as well as open-source frameworks like DeepChem and Bioconda.
